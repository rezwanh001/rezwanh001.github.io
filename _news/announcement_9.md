    <p style="font-size: 0.95rem; line-height: 1.75; color: #374151; margin-bottom: 1rem;">
      This seminar presents my MASc thesis research on multimodal depression screening using social media videos, with a particular focus on transformer-based audio-visual feature fusion. The presentation highlights two core contributions: <strong>MDD-Net</strong>, which uses a mutual transformer to fuse acoustic and visual representations, and <strong>MMFformer</strong>, which explores multiple transformer-based fusion strategies for depression detection from audiovisual content.
    </p>

    <p style="font-size: 0.95rem; line-height: 1.75; color: #374151; margin-bottom: 1.2rem;">
      The work is evaluated on the <strong>D-Vlog</strong> and <strong>LMVD</strong> datasets and demonstrates strong improvements over prior approaches, together with encouraging cross-corpus generalization results. All are welcome to attend.
    </p>
